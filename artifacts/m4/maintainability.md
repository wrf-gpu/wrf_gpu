M4 maintainability notes

The dycore remains split by responsibility: finite-difference advection, acoustic substeps, RK staging, tendency algebra, public scan wrappers, and a hand-stripped HLO sibling. Helpers exist only when they are reused by validation/tests or isolate a repeated C-grid invariant.

Tier-1 operator note: `analytic-stencil-3d-advdiff-v1` was generated by a fourth-order centered horizontal derivative, second-order centered vertical derivative, explicit diffusion, and one Euler update. The dycore operator is different, so attempt 2 adds sibling fixture `analytic-stencil-3d-upwind5-v1` with the actual dycore mass-scalar operator: 5th-order periodic horizontal upwind plus 3rd-order no-wrap vertical upwind. Tier-1 now compares `advect_mass_scalar` against that oracle.

Tier-2 uses the contract-approved simpler analytic path: nontrivial pure tracer translation with `u=5 m s-1`, `v=w=0`, zero pressure perturbation, and no acoustic forcing. This keeps the invariant proof focused on advection, mass conservation of total theta, qv positivity, finiteness, and a nonzero final-state delta. A full Straka density-current with buoyancy/hydrostatic pressure is still not implemented in M4.

Tier-3 calls the public `run(...)` dycore API on 1D periodic sine advection. The timestep is reduced from the originally suggested 20/10/5 s to 2/1/0.5 s because the unfiltered fifth-order upwind operator is unstable at the larger CFL in this reduced proxy. `JAX_ENABLE_X64=true` is set in M4 scripts to avoid precision-order sensitivity drowning the finest-grid error.

Residual physics risks: acoustic constants remain reduced-proxy constants, and the acoustic substep does not accept a separate slow-mode source term. Those are honest M4 limitations rather than performance or physical-validity claims.
