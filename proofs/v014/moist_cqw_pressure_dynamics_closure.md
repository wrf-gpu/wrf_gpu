## 1. WRF source anchoring (pristine tree `/home/enric/src/wrf_pristine/WRF`)

- `calc_cq` `dyn_em/module_big_step_utilities_em.F:856-870` — w-face moist load
  `cqw(i,k,j) = 0.5*qtot`, `qtot = Σ_species( q(k)+q(k-1) )` over the full moist
  array (qv,qc,qr,qi,qs,qg). The vertical analogue of the existing `cqu`/`cqv`
  (`acoustic_wrf.moisture_coupling_factors`).
- `pg_buoy_w` `:2474-2497` — `cq1 = 1/(1+cqw)`, `cq2 = cqw*cq1`, then
  `cqw ← cq1` (the value consumed downstream):
  - interior k: `rw_tend += (1/msfty)·g·( cq1·rdn(k)·(p(k)-p(k-1)) − c1f(k)·mu' − cq2·(c1f(k)·mub + c2f(k)) )`
  - top k=kde (uses `cqw(kde-1)`): `rw_tend += (1/msfty)·g·( cq1·2·rdnw(kde-1)·(−p(kde-1)) − c1f(kde)·mu' − cq2·(c1f(kde)·mub + c2f(kde)) )`
  - dry (`cqw=0` ⇒ `cq1=1, cq2=0`) reduces exactly to the current
    `pg_buoy_w_dry`.
- `cqw` enters the implicit W solve in `calc_coef_w`
  (`module_small_step_em.F:624-649` → `acoustic_wrf.calc_coef_w_wrf_coefficients`)
  and the `advance_w` term-A implicit pressure coefficient
  (`module_small_step_em.F:1477-1489` → `advance_w.advance_w_wrf`).

**The dry omission is purely the `−cq2·(c1f·mub + c2f)` water-MASS loading (plus
the `cq1` PGF scale).** The JAX diagnostic pressure already carries the moist
`theta_m` (`diagnose_pressure_al_alt`, `qvf=1`), so this is NOT a
virtual-temperature/EOS effect — it is the explicit weight of the water column.

## 2. The blocker = the loading term (two independent lines, h1)

### 2a. Hydrostatic-column balance (reproduces the prior PSFC proof §3 exactly)

`P+PB(k0)` minus the half-level hydrostatic pressure at k0 (Pa):

| | vs own DRY column | vs own MOIST column |
|---|---:|---:|
| GPU | **−8.18** (rides dry) | **−202.66** (off by loading) |
| CPU/WRF | +182.20 | **−13.54** (rides moist) |

`moist_col − dry_col` (integrated water loading) ≈ **194–198 Pa** (GPU 194.5 /
surface 197.5; CPU 195.7), matching the prior proof's ~200 Pa floor.

### 2b. W-equation `rw_tend` residual (the dynamics linkage, production functions)

Mean of `pg_buoy_w` `rw_tend` on the low interior faces (k=1..5), evaluated with
the production `pg_buoy_w_dry` vs `pg_buoy_w_moist` on each run's own state:

| state | DRY rw_tend mean | MOIST rw_tend mean |
|---|---:|---:|
| GPU | **−3.88 ≈ 0** (in dry balance) | **−8934** (full downward loading) |
| CPU/WRF | +8992 (dry form wrong) | **−3.12 ≈ 0** (in moist balance) |

The systematic (mean) W-balance residual confirms: the GPU state is in **dry**
hydrostatic balance, the CPU/WRF state is in **moist** balance, and the
difference is exactly the moist `pg_buoy_w` water loading. The acoustic solver
relaxes the column to whichever balance its `rw_tend` encodes — so threading the
moist term moves the GPU equilibrium **from the dry column onto the moist
column** (≈ +194 Pa at k0, closing the −202.66 → −13.5 gap).

## 3. Safety of the production change (CPU-proven)

- **Bit-identical off-path.** `pg_buoy_w_moist` with `cqw_calc=0` matches
  `pg_buoy_w_dry` to `max_abs == 0.0`, and its solver `cqw` matches `dry_cqw` to
  `max_abs == 0.0`. Dry/idealized gates (Skamarock bubble, Straka density
  current) are unchanged — they carry no moisture.
- **Implicit W solver stays well-conditioned.** `calc_coef_w` on the real GPU h1
  `mut` with moist `cqw=cq1 < 1` vs dry `cqw=1`: all coefficients finite;
  `max|γ|` 5.4808e-4 (moist) ≤ 5.4807e-4 (dry); `min α` 0.99886 (both). Moist
  `cqw<1` shrinks the off-diagonal terms → the tridiagonal is at least as
  diagonally dominant as dry. No new instability mechanism.
- **GPU-native / performance-safe.** `cqw` is computed once per RK stage from the
  stage-entry total moisture (a vertical face-average) and held constant through
  the acoustic loop — the WRF `calc_cq` cadence. No host/device transfer, no new
  full-3D transients beyond the two `(nz+1,ny,nx)` face fields the solver already
  carries, no CPU callback.
