# ADR-008 - MYNN2.5 JAX Implementation Mapping

Date: 2026-05-21
Author: M5-S2 attempt-2 worker draft (Codex gpt-5.5)
Status: ACCEPTED worker draft, pending tester/reviewer/manager closeout
Scope: implementation record for the M5-S2 dry MYNN2.5 PBL column kernel.

## Decision

Decision: replace the attempt-1 Louis/Blackadar proxy with a dry, column-only WRF MYNN2.5 transcription that is validated against a harness linked to compiled WRF `module_bl_mynnedmf` objects. The public entry point remains `step_mynn_pbl_column(state, dt, *, debug=False)` and keeps `u`, `v`, `w`, `theta`, `qv`, prognostic `tke`, `p`, `rho`, `dz`, and diagnostic `km`, `kh`, `el` leaves in fp64 (`src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:59`, `src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:504`, `src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:558`).

The implementation is intentionally dry MYNN2.5, not full EDMF mass flux. The harness disables cloud and EDMF arrays but calls real WRF MYNN entry points: `get_pblh`, `mym_turbulence`, `mym_predict`, `mynn_tendencies`, and `retrieve_exchange_coeffs` (`scripts/wrf_mynn_harness.f90:2`, `scripts/wrf_mynn_harness.f90:90`, `scripts/wrf_mynn_harness.f90:92`, `scripts/wrf_mynn_harness.f90:99`, `scripts/wrf_mynn_harness.f90:103`, `scripts/wrf_mynn_harness.f90:116`). The build links the compiled WRF objects and their module dependencies rather than a worker-authored physics routine (`scripts/wrf_mynn_harness_build.sh:11`, `scripts/wrf_mynn_harness_build.sh:12`, `scripts/wrf_mynn_harness_build.sh:13`, `scripts/wrf_mynn_harness_build.sh:57`, `scripts/wrf_mynn_harness_build.sh:58`).

## WRF Source Mapping

The WRF source defines `qke` as twice TKE and `el` as the mixing length (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:67`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:68`). The JAX kernel therefore stores prognostic TKE as `state.tke`, converts to `qke = 2*TKE` for MYNN internals, and converts back after prediction (`src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:509`, `src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:514`).

Closure constants follow the WRF MYNN block (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:278`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:309`) and are imported by the kernel (`src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:14`, `src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:47`). Level-2 gradients and flux-Richardson stability functions are transcribed from WRF `mym_level2`: WRF computes `duz`, `dtq`, `gm`, `gh`, gradient Richardson number, flux Richardson number, then `sh` and `sm` (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:1868`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:1921`). JAX implements the same dry virtual-potential path in `_mym_level2` (`src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:178`, `src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:205`).

Boundary-layer depth is the WRF hybrid theta-v/TKE diagnostic: WRF finds theta-v and TKE PBL heights and blends them with a stable-boundary-layer weight (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:5972`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:6095`). JAX implements the dry form in `_get_pblh` (`src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:223`, `src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:250`).

The option-2 master length scale follows the WRF local MYNN mixing-length branch: WRF computes `qkw`, an integrated turbulent length `elt`, a BL transition layer, buoyancy/free-atmosphere/surface component lengths, then blends them into `el` (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:2221`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:2350`). JAX implements the same dry, EDMF-off branch in `_mym_length_option2` (`src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:253`, `src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:302`).

Level-2.5 stability functions and diffusivities follow WRF `mym_turbulence`: WRF computes `q2sq`, applies the q/L stability limiter, computes the level-2.5 `e1/e2/e3/e4/eden` terms, derives `sm`/`sh`, forms `pdk`, and sets `dfm = el*q*sm/dz`, `dfh = el*q*sh/dz`, `dfq = dfm` (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:2850`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:2979`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:3188`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:3207`). JAX implements this in `_mym_turbulence` (`src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:305`, `src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:365`).

The prognostic TKE equation is handled in the WRF `mym_predict` qke solve: WRF inserts surface shear production, forms implicit coefficients, calls `tridiag2`, clamps `qke`, and reports vertical transport/dissipation budget terms (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:3421`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:3487`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:3491`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:3519`). JAX implements the same dry qke solve in `_mym_predict_qke` (`src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:409`, `src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:449`).

Mean-field vertical mixing and surface flux boundary conditions follow WRF `mynn_tendencies`: WRF puts surface momentum drag in the bottom U/V implicit system and surface heat/moisture fluxes into the bottom theta/qv RHS (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:4432`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:4592`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:4781`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:4797`). JAX uses the neutral bulk stub to produce those fluxes and applies them in `_apply_mean_tendencies` (`src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:168`, `src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:477`, `src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_pbl.py:489`).

## Flux-Richardson Radicand Decision

M5-S2.x chooses Path A: remove the JAX-only nonnegative guard around the level-2 flux-Richardson radicand. WRF computes `SQRT(ri**2 - ri3*ri + ri4)` directly inside `MIN(...)` (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:1918`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:1919`), so a negative radicand should propagate a NaN rather than silently clamp to zero. The JAX helper `_flux_richardson` now keeps that unguarded square root and the regression test constructs a discriminant-positive boundary case to show that the WRF formula and JAX both return NaN. This is a bit-match/source-faithfulness decision; no intentional defensive deviation is retained.

## Surface-Layer Coupling Interface (for M6-S3)

M5 keeps the neutral bulk placeholder, but the M6-S3 surface-layer implementation must plug in through `surface_layer(state) -> SurfaceFluxes` in `src/gpuwrf/physics/mynn_surface_stub.py`. MYNN expects these surface-layer inputs at the bottom mass level, cell-center column state sampled synchronously with the RK3 physics call:

- `ustar` (`m s-1`): friction velocity used by MYNN TKE surface production. WRF forms the qke surface production as `pdk1 = 2.0*ust**3*pmz/vkz` and assigns it into the bottom production pair (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:3421`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:3428`).
- `theta_flux` (`K m s-1`): kinematic sensible heat flux, positive upward into the atmosphere. WRF documents `flt=-u_*Theta_*` as the turbulent potential-temperature flux (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:1575`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:1578`) and uses `flt` in the bottom theta RHS (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:4589`).
- `qv_flux` (`kg kg-1 m s-1`): kinematic water-vapor flux, positive upward into the atmosphere. WRF documents `flq=-u_*qw_*` and passes `flqv` into the water-vapor bottom RHS after the negative-flux limiter (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:1575`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:1578`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:4781`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:4796`).
- `tau_u`, `tau_v` (`m2 s-2`): kinematic bottom momentum-flux components, positive upward into the atmosphere. For flow over fixed ground they are generally opposite-signed to the lowest-level wind. The current dry MYNN solve consumes the equivalent drag through `rhosfc*ust**2/wspd` in the bottom U/V diagonal (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:4436`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:4510`); `tau_u/tau_v` are kept in the interface so M6-S3 and the driver can share the signed momentum flux explicitly.
- `rhosfc` (`kg m-3`): surface density at the bottom mass level used to convert kinematic fluxes into rho-weighted tendencies. WRF computes `rhosfc` before the tendency solves (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:4311`) and multiplies scalar bottom RHS terms by `rhosfc` (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:4581`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:4589`).
- `fltv` (`K m s-1`): virtual heat flux, positive upward into the atmosphere, used by MYNN length-scale and convective terms. WRF forms it from heat and vapor fluxes before calling MYNN (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:970`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:977`) and uses `fltv` in the option-2 mixing-length branch (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:2270`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:2271`).

MYNN returns these fields to the driver: `qke_surf` (`m2 s-2`, surface TKE/qke production diagnostic derived from the WRF `pdk1` expression above) and mean-field tendency arrays `du/dt`, `dv/dt`, `dtheta/dt`, and `dqv/dt` on mass levels. WRF `mynn_tendencies` exposes `Du`, `Dv`, `Dth`, and `Dqv` as inout tendency arrays (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:4195`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:4257`) and computes them from the implicit solves (`/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:4486`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:4560`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:5334`, `/mnt/data/canairy_meteo/artifacts/wrf_gpu_src/WRF/phys/MYNN-EDMF/module_bl_mynnedmf.F90:5479`).

Time-staggering: M6-S3 should provide surface fluxes synchronously for the same RK3 substep state that MYNN advances. Lagged fluxes are not part of the M5/M6 interface unless a later ADR approves that coupling choice with stability evidence.

## Validation And Budget

Tier-1 now compares against the WRF-object-linked harness. Current parity passes for prognostic and diagnostic fields with max absolute errors `u=7.672e-4`, `v=2.805e-4`, `theta=6.281e-5`, `qv=7.273e-9`, `tke=1.460e-6`, `km=8.472e-4`, `kh=3.209e-4`, and `el=3.064e-3` (`artifacts/m5/tier1_mynn_parity.json:15`, `artifacts/m5/tier1_mynn_parity.json:24`).

Tier-2 is no longer closed-column conservation. It checks mass-weighted budgets for momentum, heat, moisture, and TKE, accounting for surface fluxes plus the prescribed-top boundary contribution and TKE production/transport/dissipation terms (`src/gpuwrf/validation/tier2_mynn.py:35`, `src/gpuwrf/validation/tier2_mynn.py:54`, `src/gpuwrf/validation/tier2_mynn.py:86`, `src/gpuwrf/validation/tier2_mynn.py:93`). Current residuals pass: momentum `3.919e-12`, heat `7.614e-11`, moisture `6.240e-11`, and TKE `9.217e-3` with a `2.0e-2` tolerance (`artifacts/m5/tier2_mynn_invariants.json:3`, `artifacts/m5/tier2_mynn_invariants.json:33`).

M5-S2.x adds an independent mean-field budget probe. The WRF harness now writes the raw `mynn_tendencies` outputs `du`, `dv`, `dth`, and `dqv`, and `tier2_mynn_independent_budget.json` compares those WRF tendency arrays against JAX one-step tendencies at the same input state. This closes the prior caveat where the momentum/heat/moisture budget check used JAX's own boundary terms on both sides.

## Consequences
