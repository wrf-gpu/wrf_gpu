# GPT MYJ/Janjic Handoff

## Objective

Port the mandatory WRF MYJ PBL + Janjic Eta surface-layer pair
(`bl_pbl_physics=2` with `sf_sfclay_physics=2`) under the frozen v0.6.0 physics
interfaces, generate fp64 WRF oracle savepoints for both halves, enforce the
pairing in registry/namelist/dispatch, and gate isolated savepoint parity
against unmodified WRF source oracles.

- `src/gpuwrf/physics/pbl_myj.py` - new MYJ PBL column port and `PhysicsStepResult`
  adapter for tendencies, TKE/mixing-length carry, and PBL diagnostics.
- `src/gpuwrf/physics/sfclay_janjic.py` - Janjic Eta surface-layer adapter fixes,
  fp64 enablement, WRF-faithful land `ZSLT` handling, and surface diagnostics.

- `src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py` - fail-closed scan-wire reason for

- `proofs/v060/oracle/myjsfc_build_and_run.sh` and

## Unresolved Risks

- These are real unmodified-WRF source oracles, not coupled `wrf.exe` real-case
  savepoints. Mixed-suite realism remains a later gate.
- MYJ `AKM/Km` is computed by the JAX port but not WRF-parity-gated here because
  unmodified `MYJPBL` does not expose the local `AKM` work array as an output
  argument. The proof records `EXCH_M/AKM/AKH` as ungated diagnostics and gates
  WRF-exported `EXCH_H/Kh`.
- The pair is registered at the frozen interface/dispatch level, but operational
  scan wiring and carry integration are explicitly fail-closed.
- No GPU performance claim is made; no GPU was used.
