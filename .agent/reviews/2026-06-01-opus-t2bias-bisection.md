## VERDICT (one line)

The d03 1 km T2 +1.5 K warm bias is **NOT land-side and NOT a surface-flux
over-flux**. It is an **atmosphere-side DIAGNOSTIC SURFACE-PRESSURE error of
~+2.6 kPa**, generated in-flight by the dycore's perturbation-pressure diagnosis
after the `force_geopotential=False` nested-boundary fix. That over-high surface
pressure inflates T2 by ~+2.0 K through the Exner conversion
`t2 = th2 * (psfc/P0)^kappa`. The underlying near-surface potential-temperature
field MATCHES the corpus to within ~0.1 K.

## Coupling map (code trace)

LSM: there is **no prognostic LSM**. `physics/noah_mp.py` is "prescribed Noah-MP
Option A" — TSK/TSLB/SMOIS are loaded from the corpus, not solved.
`integration/daily_pipeline.py::_refresh_hourly_land_state` (gated by
`refresh_land_state_hourly=True`, active for d03) **snaps `t_skin` (TSK), SST,
SMOIS, MAVAIL, roughness back to the corpus wrfout every forecast hour.** So TSK
is corpus truth; there is no skin energy-balance solve and radiation does NOT
feed TSK. (Radiation only heats the column via `rrtmg_adapter`.)

Surface→T2: `coupling/physics_couplers.py::surface_layer_diagnostics` →
`physics/surface_layer.py::surface_layer_with_diagnostics`. T2 is
`th2 = thgb + (thx - thgb)*psit2/psit`, then
`t2 = th2 * (psfcpa/P0_PA)^R_D_OVER_CP` (surface_layer.py:667,686). `thgb` is the
ground potential temp from the (corpus) TSK; `thx` is lowest-level theta.
`_surface_column_view` does NOT pass `psfc`, so `psfcpa = p1d_pa = state.p[bottom]`
(surface_layer.py:432,452). The written PSFC is the same `state.p[0]`
(`operational_mode.py::_psfc_from_state` -> `state.p[0]`).

Pressure diagnosis: `dynamics/acoustic_wrf.py::diagnose_pressure_al_alt` is a
faithful port of WRF `calc_p_rho_phi` (verified line-for-line against
`wrf_pristine/.../module_big_step_utilities_em.F:1029,1062-1086`):
`al = -(alb*c1h*mu' + rdnw*(ph'[k+1]-ph'[k]))/(c1h*muts+c2h)`; `alt = al + alb`;
`p_total = p0*(Rd*(t0+theta')/(p0*alt))^cpovcv`; `p' = p_total - pb`. The EOS form
round-trips the corpus state to 1e-10 Pa, so the formula is exact — the error
enters through `alt`, hence through the perturbation geopotential `ph'`.

## Where the pressure offset comes from (in-flight, not IC, not writer)

- IC check (`scripts/diag/ic_pressure_check.py`, GPU): at t=0 the GPU surface
  pressure EQUALS the corpus exactly (98693.59 Pa, p' = 1792.0 Pa, bias 0.0). The
  offset is **generated by the dycore during stepping**, established within the
  first forecast hour, then ~steady.
- Vertical decomposition (hour 1): PB (base) matches corpus to 0.0 Pa at every
  level; `p'` is +2.7..+3.0 kPa too high at EVERY level (near-uniform full-column
  offset). The geopotential PERTURBATION is anchored correctly at the surface
  (PH'[0] bias +0.0) and near the lid (+89 m^2/s^2), but is bowed strongly LOW in
  the column interior (k=10 bias -842, k=20 bias -1913 m^2/s^2).
- Mechanism: with `BoundaryConfig(force_geopotential=False)` (the d03 nested-
  boundary fix, `coupling/boundary_apply.py:128-131`), `ph_perturbation` /
  `ph_total` get NO lateral-boundary forcing, while u/v/w/theta/qv/mu/p ARE forced
  from the parent. The interior `ph'` relaxes to a hydrostatic balance that
  depresses the mid-column geopotential relative to corpus. Through
  `geo_term = rdnw*(ph'[k+1]-ph'[k])` -> `al` more negative -> `alt` smaller ->
  EOS `p = p0*(Rd*theta/(p0*alt))^cpovcv` inflates -> uniform +2.7 kPa `p'` offset
  -> +2 K Exner T2 inflation. The daytime `max_abs` blow-up (up to ~22 K at Teide-
  class summits) is the SAME mechanism amplified over steep terrain (T2-bias vs HGT
  correlation +0.58; bias rises monotonically with terrain height), not a separate
  surface-flux bug.

## Proposed WRF-faithful fix (NOT applied)

The `force_geopotential=False` fix correctly stopped pumping the interior with a
non-hydrostatic interpolated parent geopotential, but it threw out the baby:
leaving `ph'` completely unforced lets the boundary-ring column drift out of
hydrostatic balance with the FORCED mu'/theta', and the dycore equilibrates the
whole interior to a wrong reference -> the +2.6 kPa diagnostic-pressure offset.

Recommended (preferred) — force a HYDROSTATICALLY-CONSISTENT geopotential at the
nested boundary instead of either the raw parent value (old, pumps) or nothing
(current, drifts):
- In `coupling/boundary_apply.py`, when `force_geopotential=False` for a nested
  child, do NOT leave `ph'` untouched. Instead, RE-DERIVE the boundary-ring `ph'`
  from the FORCED mu'/theta'/qv' by hydrostatic integration on the child eta
  column (anchor at the correct surface geopotential PHB[0]; integrate
  `d(ph) = -alt * mu * d(eta)` upward using the same `alt(theta,p)` the dycore
  uses). This keeps the ring consistent with the forced thermodynamics WITHOUT
  importing the parent's non-hydrostatic interpolated `ph`. File:
  `coupling/boundary_apply.py:apply_lateral_boundaries` (the `else` branch at
  lines 128-131). Cross-check: WRF nests recompute child geopotential
  hydrostatically from the interpolated dry mass + theta at `med_interp_domain` /
  vertical-interpolation time, not by copying the parent `ph` field.

Alternative / cheaper interim — re-reference the diagnosed perturbation pressure.
Because the offset is a near-uniform column constant traceable to the `ph'`
reference, the operational PSFC and the surface-layer `psfcpa` are simply on a
wrong reference. A faithful-leaning interim is to pass the corpus/parent-consistent
surface pressure (already loaded for the boundary) into `_surface_column_view` as
`psfc` and into the writer, so T2's Exner conversion uses the physically correct
psfc rather than the drifted `state.p[0]`. This removes the +2 K T2 artifact at
the diagnostic without touching the dycore, but it does NOT fix the underlying
prognostic geopotential drift (which could still bias the interior dynamics);
treat it as a stopgap, prefer the hydrostatic-ring fix above.

Do NOT pursue any LSM/skin/HFX/MYNN change for this bias — those are confirmed
not the cause (TSK bias 0.000, theta bias ~0, HFX low not high). The P1-4a
surface-layer port is correct; it could not move this bias because the bias is
upstream in the pressure state, not in the similarity functions.
