### Bug 1 — over-water surface drag: static ZNT is the cap MAXIMUM, ~65x too rough
The d01 domain is ~90% water (4964/5487 cells). The IC/seed ocean roughness is the
WRF water value `2.85e-3` — but `2.85e-3` is only the `min()` CAP MAXIMUM in WRF's
in-run Charnock update; WRF re-derives ZNT over water from the freshly-computed
friction velocity EVERY surface-layer call (sf_sfclayrev.F90:794-833, V3.7+/Cy36r1;
MYNN charnock_1955). At this case's over-water `ustar ~= 0.121`:

| quantity (water mean)         | static seed | WRF Charnock | effect |
|-------------------------------|-------------|--------------|--------|
| ZNT                           | 2.850e-3    | **4.37e-5**  | 65x smaller |
| ustar (re-solved with new z0) | 0.1213      | 0.0834       | -31%   |
| **\|tau_v\| (surface V stress)** | 0.01484  | **0.00710**  | **-52% drag** |

The static seed over-roughens the ocean and over-drags the lowest-level wind via
`tau = -ustar^2*u/|U|` every step (the V10 deficit is surface-concentrated). The
Charnock update cuts the over-water V momentum drag in half — exactly the direction
that strengthens the meridional wind toward CPU-WRF (GPU V10 -3.45 was too weak/
positive vs CPU -4.32). NOTE: the validated d02/d03 REPLAY path ALSO held a static
ZNT and validated — but at the looser replay tolerances; the tight 0.275
foundation-match margin exposes the missing in-run update.

### Bug 2 — N/S normal-momentum boundary V forced TWICE
The native IC V at the S/N edges is bit-identical to CPU-WRF (d=0.0000), so the IC
is correct. But the per-hour boundary dV applied at the outer ring is asymmetric and
N-heavy:

| side | 1h dV (m/s) | coupled rv tendency (/s) |
|------|-------------|--------------------------|
| W    | +0.136      | +3.65 |
| E    | +0.109      | +2.81 |
| S    | +0.060      | +1.58 |
| **N**| **+0.314**  | **+8.40** |

The N edge pushes V northward ~5x harder than S. Root cause of the RESIDUAL (not the
tendency magnitude, which is the real met_em 6h->1h forcing WRF also sees): the
NORMAL momentum (u at W/E, v at S/N) is driven to the boundary value INSIDE the
acoustic loop by `apply_normal_bdy_work` (acoustic.py:457; WRF spec_zone-restricted
advance_uv + spec_bdyupdate + relax_bdy_dry on rv_tend). WRF forces the normal
momentum ONLY in the small-step loop. The pre-fix end-of-step `apply_lateral_
boundaries._apply_3d` ALSO ran spec+relax over v at ALL four sides (and u at all
four), so the v NORMAL face (S/N) got a SECOND relaxation pull toward the
(advancing-northward) boundary value every step. At the high-tendency N edge that
double pull is the residual northward V10 bias.

## root cause + fix (both WRF-faithful, no clamp/mask)

### Fix 1 — in-run Charnock ZNT over water
`physics/surface_layer.py surface_layer_with_diagnostics`: after the ustar solve,
over water (`xland>1.5`) re-derive
`znt = min(czo*ust^2/g + 0.11*1.5e-5/ust, 2.85e-3)` (czo=0.0185) and return it as
`diag.znt`. `coupling/physics_couplers.py surface_adapter` now persists `diag.znt`
to `state.roughness_m` (WRF ZNT is INTENT(INOUT) — the next step sees the evolved
ocean roughness). Land keeps its static LSM roughness (WRF only updates ZNT in the
`xland>=1.5` water branch). The Noah-MP coupler already consumed `diag.znt` over
water (noahmp_coupler.py:193) — it now correctly gets the Charnock value too.

### Fix 2 — do not double-force the NORMAL momentum at end of step
`coupling/boundary_apply.py`: new `BoundaryConfig.skip_endstep_normal_momentum`
(default `False` — keeps the validated d02/d03 replay byte-for-byte). When `True`,
`apply_lateral_boundaries` forces ONLY the TANGENTIAL velocity components at
end-of-step (u at S/N, v at W/E) via a new `sides=` filter on `_apply_3d`; the
normal faces (u@W/E, v@S/N) are left entirely to the in-loop `apply_normal_bdy_work`
— one WRF-faithful application of the normal-momentum boundary forcing per step.
Corner trimming makes each relax cell owned by exactly one side, so dropping the
normal sides leaves NO tangential-relax cell unforced (CPU-checked). The native
forecast gate (`proofs/v040/s5_forecast_gate_exec.py`) sets the flag `True` (its
in-loop normal protection is always active).
