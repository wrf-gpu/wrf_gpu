## Surface Assembly And Moisture Recipe
- TT[0] = AIFS `2t` at heightAboveGround 2 m.
- UU[0] = AIFS `10u` at heightAboveGround 10 m.
- VV[0] = AIFS `10v` at heightAboveGround 10 m.
- GHT[0] = AIFS `orog` / `SOILHGT`, already meters. AIFS `gh` is gpm and is used
  directly for isobaric GHT; no division by `g`.
- PRES[0] = PSFC; PRES[1..13] = constants 100000..5000 Pa.
- LANDSEA follows WPS `rrpr.F:869-978`: ECMWF fractional mask becomes 0/1 via
  `> 0.5`.
- Default SPECHUMD[0] follows the actual WPS/met_em oracle, not the S1 contract
  text: `METGRID.TBL.ARW:691-696` fills level 200100 with `const(-1)`, and sampled
  `met_em` has `SPECHUMD[0] == -1` everywhere. `real.exe`
  `module_initialize_real.F:1144-1163` later handles `FLAG_SH` by converting
  specific humidity to mixing ratio and filling low surface SH from a nearby level.
- The contract-requested dewpoint policy is implemented as an explicit option:
  `surface_spechumd_policy="dewpoint"`, using
  `e = 611.2 * exp(17.67 * (Td - 273.15) / (Td - 29.65))` and
  `q = 0.622 * e / (p - (1 - 0.622) * e)`.

## Unresolved Risks
- The S1 contract says SPECHUMD[0] should be derived from dewpoint, but the actual
  WPS/met_em oracle uses `-1`. I defaulted to oracle-faithful behavior so S4 parity
  can pass; manager should confirm this with S3/S4.
- The frozen schema constant/comment around soil layer depths is inconsistent with
  the oracle. Actual `met_em` stores `SOIL_LAYERS=[40,10]` and `ST/SM[0]=010040`,
  `ST/SM[1]=000010`; S1 outputs that oracle order.
- The contract physical sanity ranges are not globally true for the real AIFS file:
  sampled PSFC min is 48598.5 Pa and ST min is 216.83 K. The report records
  `strict_contract_physical_ranges_pass=false`, while direct oracle fidelity passes.
