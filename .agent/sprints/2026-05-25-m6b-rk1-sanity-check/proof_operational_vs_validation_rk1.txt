1. RK1 substep count
--------------------
operational  : `_rk_scan_step` calls `advance_stage(carry, 1.0/3.0, 1)` for RK1.
               One acoustic substep at dt_sub = (dt_s/3) / 1 = dt_s/3.
validation   : `dycore_timestep_wrf` (dycore_step.py:76) calls
               `acoustic_loop_wrf(..., substeps=int(cfg.acoustic_substeps))`
               for *every* RK stage (1, 2, 3). Default cfg.acoustic_substeps=10.
               Validation RK1 substep dt_sub = cfg.dt / 10 (acoustic_substep_wrf
               uses cfg.dt directly; see AcousticLoopConfig in acoustic_loop.py
               and AdvanceMuTInputs `dts=float(cfg.dt)`).

  DIVERGENCE A: validation RK1 runs 10 small steps; operational RK1 runs 1.
                Validation also uses dt_sub = dt/10, while operational uses
                dt_sub = dt/3.
                WRF reference (solve_em.F:1472-1475) actually specifies
                `number_of_small_timesteps = 1` for RK3-stage-1; the
                *validation* path appears to over-loop RK1 relative to the
                Fortran reference. Operational matches WRF; validation does not.
                But: WRF's RK3-stage-1 also uses `dt_rk = dt/3` and dts=dt/3 in
                stage 1 small step. Both implementations differ from WRF here:
                  - operational: substeps=1, dt_sub=dt/3 (CLOSER to WRF)
                  - validation : substeps=10, dt_sub=dt/10
                This is a known semantic divergence already documented by the
                bisection sprint and the worker report.

2. Acoustic substep operator order
----------------------------------
operational `_wrf_small_step_acoustic` order:
  a. apply_halo on incoming state
  b. compute theta perturbation (subtract _theta_base_offset)
  c. build AdvanceMuTInputs (with carry.muave, carry.muts, etc.)
  d. advance_mu_t_wrf(inputs)                  -> advanced["mu"], advanced["ww"],
                                                  advanced["mudf"], advanced["muts"],
                                                  advanced["theta"]
  e. calc_coef_w_wrf_coefficients(mu_total,...) -> a, alpha, gamma
  f. thomas_solve_scan(a, alpha, gamma, state.w) -> w_solved
  g. next_state = state.replace(w=w_solved)
     ===> ONLY `w` IS WRITTEN BACK TO PROGNOSTIC STATE  <===
     (theta and mu are explicitly NOT promoted; see comment at L307-309.)
  h. _advance_promoted_scratch updates carry scratch family
     (t_2ave, ww, muave, muts, ph_tend, u_save/v_save/w_save/t_save/ph_save/
      mu_save/ww_save).

validation `acoustic_substep_wrf` order:
  a. (no halo step inside the substep; halo is the caller's responsibility)
  b. build _advance_inputs (with state.muave, state.muts, etc.)
  c. advance_mu_t_wrf(inputs)                  -> advanced["mu"], advanced["ww"],
                                                  advanced["mudf"], advanced["muts"],
                                                  advanced["theta"]
  d. thomas_solve_scan(a, alpha, gamma, state.w) -> w_solved
     (coefficients a, alpha, gamma are computed ONCE per RK stage in
      acoustic_loop_wrf, then reused across substeps; in operational they
      are recomputed every substep — see "Coefficient cadence" below.)
  e. _ph_tend_increment(theta_old, advanced["theta"], state.ph_tend)
     ===> uses ADVANCED theta vs theta_old (i.e. the WRF small-step delta)
  f. build_scratch_state(... )
  g. state.replace(
        mu=advanced["mu"],              <== written
        mudf=advanced["mudf"],          <== written
        muts=advanced["muts"],          <== written
        muave=scratch["muave"],         <== written
        ww=scratch["ww"],               <== written
        theta=advanced["theta"],        <== written (prognostic)
        theta_ave=advanced["theta"],    <== written
        ph_tend=scratch["ph_tend"],     <== written
        w=w_solved,                     <== written
        t_2ave=scratch["t_2ave"],       <== written
     )

  DIVERGENCE B (THE PRIMARY DEFECT): operational writes ONLY `w` to prognostic
                  state. Validation writes mu, mudf, muts, theta, ph_tend, w,
                  plus scratch (theta_ave, muave, ww, t_2ave) every substep.
                  In operational, theta/mu/ww go ONLY into the resident carry's
                  scratch family via `_advance_promoted_scratch`, while the
                  prognostic theta and mu_perturbation remain at the pre-acoustic
                  candidate value (the L310 comment explicitly states this:
                  "operational prognostic theta/mu remain on the existing
                  ADR-007 state path until a separate savepoint-aligned
                  composition sprint approves replacing them").

  Concretely, in operational `_wrf_small_step_acoustic`:
    mu_new = state.mu_perturbation       <-- the pre-acoustic candidate mu,
                                             NOT advanced["mu"]
    next_state = state.replace(w=w_solved)   <-- theta unchanged

  This means the acoustic recurrence has NO effect on prognostic theta/mu
  even though the WRF small step is mathematically being run on those fields.
  The bisection report (Stage 2 table) recorded the matching deltas:
    `theta=5506.97802734375`, `mu=49.854842928446715`, `ww=15.217415205461393`.

3. Coefficient cadence
----------------------
operational : `calc_coef_w_wrf_coefficients` is computed INSIDE
              `_wrf_small_step_acoustic`, i.e. once per substep, with
              `mu_total = mu_base + state.mu_perturbation`. Because theta/mu
              are NOT promoted, mu_perturbation never changes across the
              acoustic loop, so the coefficients are effectively constant —
              but they are still recomputed every substep at runtime, which
              is wasteful.
validation  : `calc_coef_w_wrf_coefficients` is computed ONCE per RK stage in
              `acoustic_loop_wrf` (acoustic_loop.py:234-240) and reused across
              substeps. Source-faithful WRF cadence (solve_em.F:2409-2738 is
              outside the small-step loop).

  DIVERGENCE C: operational recomputes the W solver coefficients per substep
                instead of once per RK stage. Currently has no numerical
                impact because mu_perturbation is frozen, but if/when theta/mu
                promotion lands, the cadence will be wrong.

4. Halo placement
-----------------
operational : halo is applied INSIDE `_wrf_small_step_acoustic` (top of the
              function) AND wrapped around `_acoustic_scan` return and around
              each `advance_stage` return.
validation  : halo is the caller's responsibility; `acoustic_substep_wrf`
              itself does not apply a halo.

  This is not a defect — operational legitimately needs more halo touches due
  to its in-substep halo step — but it is a structural difference.

5. ph_tend semantics
--------------------
operational `_ph_tend_increment` (lines 202-211):
  `(new_state.ph - old_state.ph) / dt_sub`
  Operational uses the FINITE-DIFFERENCE of ph across the substep as the
  tendency, with units 1/dt_sub baked into the accumulation in
  `_advance_promoted_scratch` (`carry.ph_tend + increment`).

validation `_ph_tend_increment` (acoustic_loop.py:157-162):
  `increment.at[: theta_delta.shape[0], :, :].set(0.01 * theta_delta)`
  Validation uses 0.01 * (theta_new - theta_old) — a placeholder linked to the
  M6B3 acoustic recurrence anchor, not a finite-difference of ph.

  DIVERGENCE D: ph_tend formulas are completely different. This is documented
                as a known M6B3 placeholder semantic; both halves only need to
                agree on what scratch they hand to physics. But for RK1
                bitwise parity it matters.

6. Precision
------------
operational : `_enforce_operational_precision` reduces fields to operational
              dtypes (mixed-precision per ADR/perf design).
validation  : full fp64 throughout (config.update("jax_enable_x64", True);
              `jnp.asarray(..., dtype=jnp.float64)` in `_state_from_acoustic`).

  DIVERGENCE E: precision asymmetry exists but is downstream of the
                composition defect (operational does not even attempt to
                preserve theta/mu through the acoustic substep, so precision
                is moot for the failing fields).

Conclusion for Part 3
---------------------
Operational RK1 and validation RK1 are STRUCTURALLY DIVERGENT. The 879ef56 fix
correctly wires control flow into the acoustic substep at RK1, but the body of
`_wrf_small_step_acoustic` deliberately drops the theta/mu prognostic write
(L307-313). The acoustic substep therefore runs `advance_mu_t_wrf` and
`thomas_solve_scan`, but only `w` (and carry scratch) are committed; the
prognostic theta and mu_perturbation that drive RK2/RK3 advection remain at
the pre-acoustic candidate value.

This is the named gap: operational RK1 is "control-flow real" but
"state-update partial".
