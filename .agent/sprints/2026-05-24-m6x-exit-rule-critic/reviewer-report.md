# Reviewer Report - M6.x Exit-Rule Critic

## §1: Diagnosis of the new baseline numbers

The real d02 baseline is a true operator failure, not a merely noisy forecast and not a clean Tier-3/Tier-4 near miss. The run did complete and stayed resident on device, but completion is only a post-sanitize statement. The worker report records the decisive split: first post-sanitize nonfinite step is null, while the first pre-sanitize candidate nonfinite step is 2 and the transfer audit is clean at 0 post-init bytes (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/worker-report.md:32-34`). The replay proof makes the same distinction in machine-readable form: candidate nonfinites occur on 3,599 steps, first candidate nonfinite step is 2, candidate nonfinite count is 17,237,505,011, candidate clip count is 2,811,641,892, and final state leaves are finite only after the guard (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_d02_replay.json:117-124`). That is a persistent pre-sanitize blowup across essentially the whole 1h run.

The 218x T2 RMSE is therefore best read as a visible surface symptom of an internal coupled-dycore failure. The field timeline has one hourly Gen2 truth point and reports T2 RMSE 136.884916 K, U10 RMSE 106.418907 m/s, V10 RMSE 102.231934 m/s, theta_k20 RMSE 63.916530 K, and w_k20 RMSE 50.000513 m/s at 1h (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_field_rmse_timeline.json:22-51`). The replay proof additionally shows maximum absolute errors of 291.411 K for T2 and about 236 m/s for both 10 m wind components (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_d02_replay.json:5-25`). These magnitudes are far beyond a surface-layer-only mismatch. They align with the capped w, theta, u, and v limits used by the sanitizer: w is clipped to [-50, 50], theta to [150, 550], and u/v to [-150, 150] in `sanitize_state_with_stats` (`src/gpuwrf/coupling/driver.py:1011-1019`). The proof's extrema land exactly on those caps: w_abs_max 50 m/s, theta_max 550 K, theta_min 150 K, u_abs/v_abs 150 m/s (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_d02_replay.json:125-136`). The model is not producing a meteorological 1h forecast; it is running into guard rails.

The theta = 550 K finding is especially important because it survives the finite-state guard. The bound tracer reports a post-sanitize theta_K value of 550 K at step 3600 against a 400 K bound (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_bound_violation_tracer.json:12-25`). The d02 replay proof confirms theta reached the sanitizer's upper cap over the run (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_d02_replay.json:130-133`). This is not just an over-counted nonfinite statistic; it is a finite, physically impossible state after sanitization.

I do not think the leading explanation is a boundary-forcing mismatch. The run built boundary forcing from real Gen2 d02 hourly side histories for the same run used as truth (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_d02_replay.json:214-217`), and `build_replay_case` preserves the WRF P/PB, PH/PHB, and MU/MUB perturbation/base splits when loading the initial state (`src/gpuwrf/integration/d02_replay.py:354-400`). More importantly, the boundary-ring profiler shows the T2 error is not largest at the lateral boundary: 0-5 cells RMSE is 123.45 K, while the interior is 150.65 K (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_boundary_ring_error_profiler.json:16-36`). Boundary forcing can still have bugs, but this pattern points inward.

I also do not think the evidence supports "instrumentation artifact" as the primary call. The sanitizer sidecar is somewhat lossy because its `first_bad_candidate_step` has null step/time while the replay proof has step 2; that is an aggregation defect in the sidecar presentation (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_sanitizer_audit.json:11-19`, `.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_d02_replay.json:117-124`). But the underlying counts, cap hits, RMSE values, and physical-bound violation agree across independent proof files. The result is not made bad by the diagnostic. The diagnostic is exposing that the replay pass condition is too weak.

The most likely root cause class is (a): a specific operator bug or tightly localized source-equation mismatch inside the vertical acoustic / mass-continuity coupling. I do not mean "one typo is certain." I mean the failure is early, saturating, and equation-shaped enough that a bug hunt should precede a full architecture pivot. The current code still has a load-bearing, explicitly unsourced `_mu_continuity_increment` tanh cap, preserved because the public scan becomes nonfinite without it (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/acoustic_wrf.py:473-495`). The same acoustic step applies the MPAS-family vertical recurrence, then applies mu continuity after the vertical solve (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/acoustic_wrf.py:911-930`). That ordering is a plausible failure site for an unstable coupling between w/theta/rho and column mass. Separately, the MPAS recurrence RHS signs and coefficient dimensions are concentrated in a small block (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/acoustic_wrf.py:763-827`, `src/gpuwrf/dynamics/vertical_implicit_solver.py:18-82`). The vertical phase-space proof showing sampled columns jumping to w = 50 m/s at boundary, terrain, and ocean proxies strengthens the suspicion of a global operator-scale error rather than a local terrain or boundary bug (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_vertical_column_phase_space.json:62-80`, `.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_vertical_column_phase_space.json:131-148`, `.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_vertical_column_phase_space.json:200-217`).

Category (c), fundamental architectural inadequacy of the conservative column solver plus small carry, remains a live risk, but it is not yet proven. The source-mining table says only one row is a correct target shape, five rows are approximate or blocked, and two rows are missing accepted production evidence (`.agent/decisions/source_mining_operator_table.md:20-24`). ADR-023 itself remains PROPOSED and requires reviewer concurrence (`.agent/decisions/ADR-023-conservative-column-solver.md:1-3`). That is a serious architecture warning. But because the failure could still be caused by a sign, unit, staging, or missing WRF/MPAS coefficient in a small number of lines, the next decision should be a bounded bug hunt, not an immediate third-path scout.

## §2: Is S3-real plausibly sufficient?

S3-real is not plausibly sufficient if it is dispatched as written in `s3_input_memo_real.md`. The memo lists three operator concerns: replace `_mu_continuity_increment`, replace the `MPAS_OMEGA_TO_W_METRIC = 1.35` constant, and demote the `MPAS_COLUMN_BUOYANCY_TENDENCY_SCALE = 0.38` scalar (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/s3_input_memo_real.md:3-40`). But the current code already shows the latter two fixes in production form. `_mpas_w_metric_faces` replaces the former synthetic scalar with a per-column/per-level metric except for analytic-slice compatibility (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/acoustic_wrf.py:512-534`), and the real non-analytic path uses `SOURCE_BACKED_COLUMN_BUOYANCY_TENDENCY_SCALE` rather than the slice-only 0.38 (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/acoustic_wrf.py:919-923`). The S3-narrow worker report states the same: production 1.35 was removed, 0.38 was demoted to slice-only, and `_mu_continuity_increment` was deliberately deferred (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-23-m6x-s3narrow-stabilizer-source-cleanup/worker-report.md:7-13`).

That matters because the catastrophic baseline appears to be after those source-cleanup changes. If so, two thirds of the named S3-real correction package have already failed to prevent the 1h d02 collapse. The remaining mandatory work is the mass-continuity cap, plus any sourced damping or coefficient correction discovered during the sprint. Replacing `_mu_continuity_increment` is necessary for governance: the function says WRF and MPAS do not have this tanh dry-column cap and that it is preserved only because the warm-bubble path becomes nonfinite without it (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/acoustic_wrf.py:480-488`). The source table also says the minimum fix is to replace or ratify the mass update against WRF MUAVE/MUTS/ww or MPAS perturbation-state lines, and explicitly not to substitute Rayleigh damping for mass continuity (`.agent/decisions/source_mining_operator_table.md:11`). But necessary is not the same as sufficient.

The current proof package does not show that the mu limiter is the dominant error source. The limiter sidecar says `limiter_active: false` and has an empty per-step table (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_limiter_activation_tracker.json:11-15`), while the findings file notes that raw/bounded dmu arrays are not exposed by the preserved proof (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/findings_real.md:58-64`). The operator term-budget sidecar is even less decisive: every named term is `present: false`, and the status is `NO_TERMS` (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_operator_term_budget_tracer.json:11-80`). The summary admits both missing telemetry gaps: raw dmu/bounded dmu arrays and per-substep RHS arrays are not serialized (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_s2dot1redo_summary.json:202-207`). So S3-real would be operating on a plausible suspect, not confirmed dominance.

Could a correct WRF/MPAS mass update improve T2 RMSE from 136 K to about 3 K in one or two sprints? It is possible only if the current mu update is causing the step-2 nonfinite cascade and the rest of the vertical recurrence is basically right. The available evidence does not support betting the next sprint on that assumption. The conservation tracker reports a 477.68 relative drift in the surface wind energy proxy and a 9.1 percent drift in surface temperature sum over its two stored states (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_conservation_tracker.json:11-16`, `.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_conservation_tracker.json:34-49`). The sampled w columns all hit the sanitizer cap. The surface error is interior-heavy. This looks broader than a gentle mass limiter bias.

My judgement: S3-real should not be cancelled, but it should be reframed. Do not dispatch a "replace the three known items" cleanup sprint. Dispatch a bug-hunt sprint whose first task is to prove or disprove whether the remaining mu cap, MPAS recurrence signs/coefficients, or RK/acoustic staging is the first source of the step-2 nonfinite. If the bug hunt identifies a concrete fix, that fix can become S3-real's implementation payload. If it cannot identify a concrete cause quickly, blind S3-real is unlikely to bridge a two-orders-of-magnitude RMSE gap.

## §3: Exit-rule status

The formal HYBRID exit rule should not fire yet, but the project is at the warning threshold immediately before it fires. The prior critic's rule was explicit: after S2-S4, allow one cleanup/fix sprint if sidecars localize the failure; after that require a manager decision memo (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-23-m6x-close-strategy-plan-critic/reviewer-report.md:112-116`). The same report recommended S6 closeout or explicit architecture exit if no temporary stabilizer remains and Tier-3/Tier-4 pass, otherwise a bounded choice among WRF scratch hybrid, full WRF small-step, or third-path substrate scout (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-23-m6x-close-strategy-plan-critic/reviewer-report.md:47-49`). We have the no-change real d02 baseline. We do not yet have a completed S3-real replacement or a Tier-3 controlled convergence run; the timestep dashboard is structure-only and explicitly pending S4 (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6x-s2dot1redo-real-baseline/proof_timestep_convergence_dashboard.json:11-22`).

ADR-021 is effectively unavailable as a clean immediate fallback. The clamp-strip honest test became nonfinite at step 2, with theta perturbation beyond +/-21,800 K and signed vertical velocity around +/-1.5e8 m/s at step 1 (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-23-m6x-adr021-clamp-strip-honest-test/worker-report.md:95-115`). That closes the easy "just return to ADR-021" branch. ADR-023 is also not healthy: it remains PROPOSED, its warm-bubble unified path had unresolved mu-stabilization risk, and ADR-024 explicitly says it does not ratify any operator architecture or remove the mu limiter (`.agent/decisions/ADR-023-conservative-column-solver.md:163-175`, `.agent/decisions/ADR-024-warm-bubble-gate-policy.md:41-45`).

Still, I would not write `M6-DYCORE-BLOCKER-MEMO` today. The current evidence is sufficient to bar M6 green, but not sufficient to prove that ADR-023's small-carry conservative-column architecture cannot pass after a localized correction. The proof package localizes the failure to "inside the coupled ADR-023 replay" rather than "boundary only," but it does not expose the term arrays needed to distinguish a sign/unit/staging bug from a structural missing-state problem. The fair exit-rule interpretation is:

- Current status: pre-exit-rule, but red.
- Allowed next step: one bounded operator bug hunt that can produce either a concrete fix or a blocker memo.
- Hard stop: if that bug hunt cannot produce a sanitizer-free short replay improvement, or if the concrete fix requires broad carry expansion / WRF scratch families, then the exit rule fires and the manager should write the blocker memo rather than spending more sprints tuning stabilizers.

This interpretation preserves the critic-ratified HYBRID plan without letting "S3-real has not been tried" become an excuse for open-ended dycore churn.

## §4: Recommended next action

Recommendation: DISPATCH-OPERATOR-BUG-HUNT

Do not dispatch S3-real as a normal cleanup sprint. Dispatch a short operator bug-hunt sprint with frozen scope: no architecture promotion, no new hidden stabilizers, no target-shaped damping, and no acceptance based on post-sanitize finiteness. The objective is to find or disprove a concrete operator bug that explains the step-2 candidate nonfinites and cap-saturated 1h state. The sprint may implement a fix only if it can point to the exact source-equation mismatch, sign/unit error, staging error, or missing time-averaged term and then show an A/B improvement on a pre-sanitize diagnostic. If it cannot, the next deliverable should be `M6-DYCORE-BLOCKER-MEMO`, not another cleanup sprint.

The sprint should use two proof rungs. First, a very short replay rung: 1, 2, 5, and 10 steps with sanitizer disabled or converted to abort-on-first-bad. This rung should not touch physics acceptance, RMSE thresholds, or Tier-3 wording. Its output is simply first bad field, first bad substep, first bad term, and whether the bad value appears before or after the vertical recurrence, mu update, physics, or boundary application. Second, a localized A/B rung: run the same short replay with exactly one suspect toggled at a time. The manager should reject any report that changes multiple suspect mechanisms and then claims the result is localized.

The largest likely gain is not from the already-demoted 0.38 scalar or already-replaced 1.35 metric. The largest likely gain is from either a wrong sign/dimension in the MPAS recurrence, a broken w/rw metric scale, a mu-continuity update applied at the wrong time/state, or a dt/acoustic substep staging mismatch that makes the vertical recurrence unstable under the real d02 state. The bug hunt should first run a 1-10 step sanitize-off or abort-on-first-bad replay and capture the first bad variable, first bad location, and per-term RHS arrays before the guard. Then it should run tiny A/B toggles: disable physics after the initial state, disable mu continuity, vary n_acoustic, and compare nonhydrostatic recurrence against the pressure-scale positive branch on the same d02 slice. Those are diagnostics, not acceptance gates.

The reason I prefer this over immediate S3-real is evidentiary hygiene. The S3-real fix list currently mixes one truly pending item with two items that appear already landed in current code. If the next sprint changes `_mu_continuity_increment`, reworks damping, adjusts coefficients, and reports a different 1h RMSE, the manager still may not know which mechanism mattered. The bug-hunt shape forces the first-bad path to be identified before implementation. That is the cheapest way to avoid both false hope and premature exit.

Worst-case fallback is clear. If the bug hunt shows the failure requires WRF `MUAVE/MUTS/ww/t_2ave` scratch state or a Newton/nonlinear HEVI extension outside ADR-023's six-leaf carry, write the formal blocker memo with bounded options: WRF scratch hybrid, full WRF small-step with savepoint harness, or third-path substrate scout. If it finds a simple sign/unit bug and a fix brings the short replay off the sanitizer caps, continue HYBRID through S3-real and then S4 Tier-3.

## §6: Open questions for the manager

The evidence that would change my recommendation toward `CONTINUE-HYBRID-WITH-S3-REAL` is a pre-sanitize, per-step trace showing the first bad value originates in `_mu_continuity_increment` or one other named term, plus an A/B run where a source-backed replacement removes step-2 nonfinites and knocks the 1h surface RMSE down by at least an order of magnitude without new caps. The current proofs do not show that. They show sanitizer masking, cap hits, and missing term telemetry.

The evidence that would change my recommendation toward `DISPATCH-THIRD-PATH-SCOUT` or `M6-DYCORE-BLOCKER-MEMO` is equally concrete: a bug hunt that shows the MPAS recurrence signs/coefficients are faithful, the metric is correctly scaled, mu continuity is not the first failure, and the remaining fix requires broad WRF scratch/time-averaged state that ADR-023 forbids. ADR-023 explicitly keeps public carry to six leaves and says no local or scratch field may be added to `AcousticScanCarry` without a new ADR (`.agent/decisions/ADR-023-conservative-column-solver.md:62-71`). If the proof says those missing state families are necessary, the exit rule fires.

Additional diagnostics I would ask from the S1 sidecars before any architecture decision:

- First-bad pre-sanitize tensor dump for steps 1-5: field, location, value, and immediately preceding state.
- Raw and bounded dmu arrays, not only aggregate limiter status.
- Operator term budget arrays for pressure restoring, density coupling, buoyancy, theta transport, smdiv, Rayleigh, boundary forcing, and mu continuity.
- A 10-step n_acoustic and dt sweep with sanitize-off abort behavior.
- A boundary-disabled or physics-disabled short run to separate dycore instability from physics/surface feedback.
- One d02 column coefficient dump from `build_epssm_column_coefficients`, compared numerically to the MPAS/WRF source equations.

The manager should also decide in advance what counts as a successful bug-hunt result. My suggested bar is not "1h RMSE passes" in the bug-hunt sprint. It is: first candidate nonfinite step moves from 2 to null for at least a 10-step sanitize-off replay, no field lands on sanitizer caps in a guarded replay, and the report names the changed equation with source citations. Anything less is useful diagnostic data, but it is not permission to keep tuning.

Closing recommendation: treat the real 1h baseline as a red architecture warning, but spend exactly one bounded sprint on operator-bug disproof before writing the blocker memo. The manager should not accept a "finite after sanitize" proof, should not let S3-real repeat already-landed metric/buoyancy cleanup, and should not promote ADR-023 until the next proof is sanitizer-free for the short replay and can explain the first-bad term.
