# Architecture Step-Back Critic Report

## 1. Bottom line

Continue c2, but define it as a WRF-formula, JAX-native dycore architecture, not a literal line-by-line Fortran port. The c2 architecture is the right container: `BaseState`, `BoundaryState`, perturbation/total state leaves, and `DycoreMetrics` directly address the failure modes c1 exposed; the current c2 failure is that the acoustic implementation is still missing WRF's vertical momentum/geopotential/theta transport, not that the architecture should be scrapped. Revisit no later than May 24, 2026 or after one c2-A2.x rescue sprint, whichever comes first.

## 3. Detailed assessment per candidate

**c2-continue.** This is the best path because the architecture now represents the missing WRF quantities instead of patching them into c1. `DycoreMetrics` carries map factors, hybrid-eta coefficients, interpolation coefficients, terrain slopes, and `p_top` as static device-resident leaves (`src/gpuwrf/contracts/grid.py:56-94`, `:111-139`), while `State` has first-class total and perturbation pressure/geopotential/mu fields (`src/gpuwrf/contracts/state.py:327-351`) and `BaseState` separates `pb`, `phb`, `mub`, `t0`, and `theta_base` (`src/gpuwrf/contracts/state.py:99-167`). That matches WRF's actual small-step dependencies: small-step prep/finish converts variables into and out of coupled perturbation form (`module_small_step_em.F:125-274`, `:377-430`), pressure is diagnosed from `al/alt/theta/ph/mu` with smdiv memory (`:522-563`), horizontal PGF uses coupled `ph/p/pb/al/alt/mu` terms (`:828-868`, `:902-942`), mass/theta/omega are updated inside the acoustic loop (`:1094-1171`), and vertical `w/ph` needs the tridiagonal path (`calc_coef_w` at `:620-648`, `advance_w` at `:1477-1584`). The current c2 implementation covers PGF and in-loop mu continuity (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/acoustic_wrf.py:309-408`) but its substep only updates `u`, `v`, Rayleigh-damped `w`, and `mu` (`:457-487`), which explains the c2-A2 proof failure: first nonfinite at step 1 and no warm-bubble rise (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-22-m6x-c2-A2-pgf-acoustic-implementation/worker-report.md:31-47`). Continue, but make the next sprint a vertical-acoustic completion gate, not another broad patch set.

**Dinosaur-style spectral JAX.** Dinosaur is attractive as a JAX style reference, not as the project dycore. Its README says it solves shallow-water and primitive equations with spectral methods on sigma coordinates and is built for accelerator/JAX differentiability (`Dinosaur@59a0197:README.md:5-10`). Its `CoordinateSystem` is clean, with horizontal/vertical grid objects and explicit physics vs dycore sharding (`dinosaur/coordinate_systems.py:84-157`), modal/nodal pytree conversions (`:202-343`), pytree filters (`dinosaur/filtering.py:32-100`), and IMEX time integration abstractions (`dinosaur/time_integration.py:74-114`, `:193-226`). But the same evidence shows the mismatch: global spectral/sigma coordinates are not a regional WRF C-grid/hybrid-eta limited-area dycore with `wrfbdy` forcing. It would likely avoid the exact step-1 c2 bug by replacing the whole numerical family, but it would create a new validation problem and make Tier-4 WRF RMSE compatibility hard to interpret.

**ICON4Py-style C-grid unstructured.** ICON4Py is the strongest modern evidence that a Python/GT4Py nonhydrostatic dycore should carry explicit configs, metric state, interpolation state, and intermediates. `NonHydrostaticConfig` names pressure-gradient method, Rayleigh type, divergence damping order/type, off-centering, and damping-height bands (`ICON4Py@3934f68:model/atmosphere/dycore/solve_nonhydro.py:139-243`). `SolveNonhydro` owns `metric_state_nonhydro`, `interpolation_state`, `vertical_params`, geometry, and exchange runtime (`:338-365`), wires divergence damping and the vertically implicit solver through explicit constant arguments (`:463-609`), and allocates named local fields including nonhydro buoyancy, hydrostatic correction, Rayleigh factor, and fourth-order divergence damping (`:787-911`). Its states enumerate time stepping, divergence damping, and pressure discretization choices (`dycore_states.py:35-87`), and its divergence damping has a dedicated vertical-wind derivative stencil (`add_vertical_wind_derivative_to_divergence_damping.py:16-60`). This supports c2's data taxonomy, but copying ICON numerics would be a larger rewrite onto unstructured ICON geometry, not a fast path to WRF-compatible Canary forecasts.

**Pace/FV3.** Pace is the best precedent for a production Python/GPU dycore decomposition. `DynamicalCore` owns `grid_data`, `damping_coefficients`, `config`, `state`, `timestep`, and checkpointer hooks (`Pace@6a46e69:fv3core/pace/fv3core/stencils/fv_dynamics.py:92-123`); it composes acoustic dynamics, hyperdiffusion, remapping, tracer transport, and negative adjustment as named modules (`:257-293`, `:488-609`). Its `AcousticDynamics` owns grid data, damping coefficients, vertical solvers, shallow-water operators, nonhydrostatic pressure gradient, hyperdiffusion, Rayleigh damping, halo updaters, and an acoustic loop (`dyn_core.py:345-590`, `:701-936`). `GridData` and `DampingCoefficients` make metric and damping data first-class (`util/pace/util/grid/helper.py:20-42`, `:45-87`, `:124-237`, `:306-330`), while `HyperdiffusionDamping`, `RayleighDamping`, and `FillNegativeTracerValues` are standalone modules (`del2cubed.py:78-194`, `ray_fast.py:144-206`, `fillz.py:14-163`). Pace therefore argues for c2's architecture, not for an FV3 replacement: FV3 uses a different grid and vertical-remap family, so a direct pivot would be expensive and RMSE compatibility against WRF would become a model-to-model comparison rather than a port validation.

**Minimal regional JAX dycore from scratch.** This has the cleanest implementation surface and may get a stable warm-bubble fastest if the goal is only "a model that runs." But dropping WRF hybrid-eta, WRF split-explicit structure, and WRF-compatible perturbation/base-state contracts would abandon the strongest available validation oracle exactly when the project needs evidence. The c1 history shows why: isolated bugs were fixed repeatedly, including horizontal momentum finite through 360 steps and later vertical momentum finite through 360 steps, yet 1h coupled remained red at 43.90-44.33x speedup (`c1-a7 worker-report.md:36-45`, `c1-a8 worker-report.md:45-61`), and sanitize-based 24h acceptance failed Tier-4 by a 21.26 ratio against a <=1.5 target (`m6x-option-b manager-closeout.md:1-25`). Minimal-from-scratch reduces same-bug recurrence, but it also removes the WRF fixture path that can tell us whether the result is meteorologically compatible.

**Hybrid c2 with non-WRF acoustic component.** This is the right fallback if c2-A2.x fails the pivot criteria below. The survivable hybrid is not "scrap all of c2"; it is "keep c2 state, metrics, fixtures, and validation, then replace the failing vertical acoustic/split-explicit component with a clean JAX vertical-implicit or IMEX operator." Dinosaur's IMEX abstractions show the JAX formulation style is practical (`dinosaur/time_integration.py:74-114`), while WRF and ICON/Pace show the minimum variables the replacement still has to respect: base/perturbation pressure/geopotential/mu, vertical buoyancy, divergence damping, Rayleigh, and metric-aware mass flux. This option has better speed odds than a full external dycore pivot and better RMSE measurability than a total rewrite, but it should require an ADR because it weakens WRF-identical small-step parity.

## 4. Pivot criteria

Continue c2 through exactly one focused rescue sprint, c2-A2.x, with this stop/go gate:

- By May 24, 2026, c2-A2.x must implement the WRF vertical acoustic path equivalent to `advance_w`, `advance_mu_t` theta/omega terms, and the `calc_coef_w` tridiagonal coefficient/solve path, with line-cited formulas and tests against an analytic oracle or WRF savepoint.
- The warm-bubble proof must no longer fail at large step 1; it must run 600 s finite, show nonzero vertical response, and report no sanitize-based acceptance.
- A WRF-source residual proof must show the vertical `w/ph/theta` subterms are not merely plausible but numerically tied to `module_small_step_em.F:1178-1584` and `:1094-1171`.
- The transfer audit must still show no host/device transfer inside timestep loops. Any performance statement must remain non-claim unless profiler or transfer artifacts exist.
- If c2-A2.x still has first nonfinite at step 1, cannot produce a vertical-acoustic oracle, or needs broad unreviewed state/contract changes, pivot immediately to the hybrid path and write an ADR before implementation.

Secondary pivot trigger: if c2 passes warm-bubble but cannot reach 1h coupled finite within two additional focused sprints, pivot to hybrid. Do not pivot to Dinosaur, ICON4Py, or Pace unless the user explicitly relaxes WRF-compatibility as a Tier-4 requirement.

## 5. What survives if we pivot

The following should survive any sane pivot: ADR-001 JAX-primary backend, ADR-002 SoA C-grid state layout, fp64 pressure/mass/geopotential policy, `State`/`BaseState`/`BoundaryState`, `GridSpec.metrics`/`DycoreMetrics`, Gen2 and WRF fixture infrastructure, warm-bubble/Schar/1h/24h proof harnesses, transfer-audit rules, physics adapters, public forecast driver shape, and the governance rule that no physics or performance claim closes without proof.

What gets rewritten in a hybrid pivot: `src/gpuwrf/dynamics/acoustic_wrf.py` internals, vertical acoustic solver, acoustic scan carry, and orchestration around mass/theta/geopotential updates. What gets rewritten in a full Dinosaur/ICON/Pace pivot: essentially the whole dycore numerical core plus boundary mapping, validation baselines, and much of the state-to-dycore interface; the existing physics and Gen2 IO might survive only through adapters.

## 6. Open questions for the council

- Is the project's near-term definition of WRF compatibility "same equations/savepoint-close enough" or "operational RMSE close enough even if numerics differ"? This decides whether hybrid is allowed after c2-A2.x.
- Do we have a WRF savepoint exposing enough `advance_w` and `advance_mu_t` intermediates to make the next c2 gate anti-tautological, or must we build a smaller Fortran harness first?
- Should c2 use WRF's exact split-explicit/off-centering sequence, or may it use a JAX IMEX equivalent once warm-bubble stability is proven?
- What is the acceptable latency cost of a vertical tridiagonal solve inside the acoustic scan before the 4x constitutional floor is at risk?
- Can the manager authorize a fast ADR if c2-A2.x fails, so the hybrid pivot does not repeat c1's unbounded patch loop?
