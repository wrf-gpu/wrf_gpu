# §1 — Defect-cascade diagnosis

The defect cascade is stronger evidence for structural divergence than for a normal queue of isolated bugs. The first M6b attempt already showed that the operational strict-subset theory was under-specified: all three real Gen2 d02 runs failed after one 10 s step, with theta maxima from `1.879e13` to `1.422e15` K and vertical-wind maxima up to `7.114e14` m/s (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-honest-1h-canary/worker-report.md:11`, `.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-honest-1h-canary/worker-report.md:17-19`). The carry-expansion sprint then promoted `t_2ave`, `ww`, `muave`, `muts`, `ph_tend`, and `_save` from undecided to operational-required, while explicitly avoiding imports of `acoustic_loop`, `dycore_step`, `coupled_step`, or `small_step_scratch` (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-fix-carry-expansion/worker-report.md:15-22`, `.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-fix-carry-expansion/worker-report.md:26-29`). That fixed the 10 s probe but not the 1 h gate: the RETRY still failed theta bounds at 100 s, 350 s, and 620 s (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-honest-1h-canary-RETRY/worker-report.md:17-21`, `.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-honest-1h-canary-RETRY/worker-report.md:46-48`).

The next fixes did not converge like a short tail of local defects. Bisection found that operational RK1 skipped the acoustic loop, while validation applied `acoustic_substep_wrf` to the same RK1 candidate and immediately changed `theta`, `mu`, `ww`, `w`, and scratch (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-operational-composition-bisection/worker-report.md:9-12`, `.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-operational-composition-bisection/worker-report.md:27-38`). The RK1 fix was real but partial: it added one RK1 acoustic small step and preserved B6 validation parity at `max_abs_delta: 0.0`, yet 70 s remained bounds-red and the worker warned that theta/mu promotion was still not solved (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-fix-rk1-acoustic-loop/worker-report.md:9-14`, `.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-fix-rk1-acoustic-loop/worker-report.md:67-71`). The RK1+D2H acceptance then proved the D2H lift, but RK1 parity failed at step 1 on all four wrfout-rich Gen2 IDs, again with theta as the largest diverging field (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-rk1-d2h-acceptance/worker-report.md:11-13`, `.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-rk1-d2h-acceptance/worker-report.md:36-45`).

The sanity and real-IC bisection reports are the decisive evidence. The sanity memo found operational RK1 now enters `_wrf_small_step_acoustic`, but the body had not been semantically identical to validation: `advance_mu_t_wrf` outputs were computed but not promoted into prognostic theta/mu, with secondary deltas in coefficient cadence, RK1 `dt_sub`, halo placement, `ph_tend`, and precision (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-rk1-sanity-check/sanity_memo.md:52-87`). The real-IC bisection independently named `advance_mu_t_committed_outputs` as first bad operator and cited the missing commits for `advanced["mu"]`, `advanced["theta"]`, and `advanced["mudf"]` (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-real-ic-bisection/worker-report.md:9-22`). The four-defect fix applied those repairs but still failed with a step-1 delta of `5.62949953421312e14` and a nonfinite 10 s probe; the worker then named a fifth defect, raw `state.u/state.v` fed where WRF passes `ru_m/rv_m/ww_m` (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-fix-advance-mu-t-commit/worker-report.md:5-12`, `.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-fix-advance-mu-t-commit/worker-report.md:20-30`).

The honest stay-the-course reading is that the defects are being localized with increasingly precise bisection, and the validation ladder remains bitwise-locked. B6 coupled-step parity passed column, patch16, and golden tiers for 10 timesteps with `0.0` max observed delta (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b6-coupled-step-parity/worker-report.md:17-24`). The operational path also achieved warmed `d2h_inter_kernel=0` after static sequencing (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-rk1-d2h-acceptance/worker-report.md:11`, `.agent/decisions/ADR-027-d2h-invariant-clarification-PROPOSED.md:36-46`). But this is no longer a two-defect tail. The sequence shows that every time operational is made more WRF-shaped, a new interface mismatch appears at the next semantic boundary. That is the signature of two independently composed timesteps, not of one shared numerical core with mode-specific wrappers.

The practical cost of this pattern is not only time. Each defect sprint also changes what “operational” means. Strict subset became promoted scratch; promoted scratch became partial prognostic commit; partial commit became full commit; full commit exposed mass-coupled momentum. The operational design is therefore being inferred from failure deltas after the fact, not derived from a frozen numerical contract. That is the opposite of the “freeze interfaces before parallel work begins” operating rule in `AGENTS.md:18`, and it makes every apparent closeout provisional.

# §2 — The 'compose own variants' rule

Amendment #1 was the right rule when drafted. The principal directive says a correct-but-GPU-inefficient CPU-shaped solution is wrong for the project, and explicitly separates bitwise savepoint parity from operational mode: operational must drop scratch, fuse boundaries, downcast only where authorized, and choose GPU-friendly solvers (`/home/enric/.claude/projects/-home-enric-src-wrf-gpu2/memory/feedback_gpu_optimized_core_primacy.md:10-17`). PROJECT_PLAN §14.5.1 encodes the same distinction: validation mode carries WRF scratch, emits savepoints, uses fp64 and serial Thomas; operational mode is a strict GPU-optimized variant with fused operators, zero H2D/D2H, profiler-selected solver, and diagnostics eliminated from production (`PROJECT_PLAN.md:254-266`). The failure mode Amendment #1 was preventing was real: validation savepoint code could become production runtime shape merely because it was the only tested shape.

The speed-vs-bitwise critic also did not endorse a naive single path. It recommended “two-mode-distinct” with shared pure numerical kernels underneath: validation owns savepoint emission and strict parity, operational owns fused scans, carry subset, precision policy, and solver selection (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-24-m6-speed-vs-bitwise-critic/reviewer-report.md:43-51`). That remains the correct architectural principle.

What has changed is the empirical status of “operational composes its own variants.” It has not produced a strict subset; it produced a second WRF-shaped composer that is now carrying most of the originally forbidden scratch family. `OperationalCarry` contains `t_2ave`, `ww`, `mudf`, `muave`, `muts`, `ph_tend`, and seven save-family leaves (`src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_state.py:31-59`). Its evidence table says those fields were promoted because strict-subset operational failed (`src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_state.py:18-26`). Operational mode now imports the same numerical building blocks (`calc_coef_w_wrf_coefficients`, `advance_mu_t_wrf`, and `thomas_solve_scan`) but still hand-copies composition around them (`src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:23-28`, `src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:260-340`). In other words, the rule succeeded at preventing imports of validation wrappers, but failed at preventing CPU-shape creep and also failed at preserving validated composition.

The failure mode is still real; the discipline is not moot. Directly making `coupled_step.py` production would import validation assumptions: the module declares itself validation-only and not imported by operational runtime (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/coupled_step.py:1-6`), and ADR-026 still says all M6B validation helpers remain validation-only (`.agent/decisions/ADR-026-operational-mode-design-PROPOSED.md:59-61`). However, the current rule is over-broad. It bans reuse of composition that has the only bitwise evidence, while allowing manual transcription that copies the same scratch semantics without the same proof. The right supersession is not “validation helpers are production”; it is “validation and operational import a shared pure core, while savepoint and production concerns live outside that core.”

The stay-the-course defense is that this is how operational escape hatches are discovered: start from a lean GPU path, add only what failures prove necessary, and keep validation code out until Tier-4 forces the issue. That defense would be compelling if the fixes were preserving a materially different GPU design. They are not. The current operational file now contains the validation scratch families, the WRF-shaped Thomas solver, and WRF small-step recurrence calls, but it lacks the protection of the validation composition tests at each copied boundary (`src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:216-340`). That is a poor trade: the project pays the correctness risk of divergence without yet receiving the performance benefit of a truly different core.

# §3 — Pilot-vs-core trade

The current `coupled_step.coupled_timestep_wrf` is not itself an operational core. It is a validation wrapper around WRF-ordered dycore, physics, and boundary composition: it rejects non-RK3 or disabled physics/boundary, calls `dycore_timestep_wrf`, applies physics, applies boundary, and returns a snapshot dictionary (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/coupled_step.py:232-253`). It also builds physical `State` objects for physics by adding theta and pressure offsets (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/coupled_step.py:111-176`). As written, it materializes snapshots and uses Python-loop validation helpers underneath, so importing it directly would be a pivot to validation-as-operational, not a clean GPU core.

But its transitive numerical operators are mostly optimizable. `advance_mu_t_wrf` is already used directly by operational mode (`src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:25`, `src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:270-304`). `thomas_solve_scan` is already a JAX `lax.scan` forward/back sweep, so it can be used in validation, operational, and later PCR/batched-Thomas substitution without changing call semantics (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/tridiag_solve.py:13-29`, `src/gpuwrf/dynamics/tridiag_solve.py:32-62`). `acoustic_loop_wrf` computes coefficients once and iterates substeps in Python for validation snapshots (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/acoustic_loop.py:224-246`); that loop can be split into a pure scan body plus an optional savepoint collector. `dycore_timestep_wrf` similarly wraps three RK stages in a Python loop and appends snapshots (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/dycore_step.py:65-85`); the operational equivalent already proves the RK/acoustic shape can be expressed as `lax.scan` or static sequencing (`src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:343-393`). Physics and boundary adapters are pure state transforms at this layer; their operational concern is cadence, offsets, precision, and segmentation, not a different physics formula (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/coupled_step.py:188-229`, `src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:396-416`).

Call-site judgment: `dycore_timestep_wrf` is optimizable if its RK loop becomes a static schedule or scan and its `rk_snapshots` list is moved to a validation wrapper (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/dycore_step.py:73-85`). `acoustic_loop_wrf` is optimizable if coefficient cadence, substep count, and snapshot collection become explicit core outputs or wrapper policies rather than hidden Python control flow (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/acoustic_loop.py:233-246`). `acoustic_substep_wrf` is the best shared-core candidate because it already composes the exact fields that repeatedly diverged: `advanced["mu"]`, `mudf`, `muts`, `muave`, `ww`, `theta`, `ph_tend`, `w`, and `t_2ave` (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/acoustic_loop.py:175-214`). `coupled_timestep_wrf` itself is least suitable for direct import because it returns a snapshot dictionary and hard-requires physics and boundary enabled (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/coupled_step.py:242-253`). `thomas_solve_scan` is already core-shaped; the operational wrapper can later swap solver implementation behind the same interface if ADR-026/ADR-007 evidence permits (`PROJECT_PLAN.md:263`, `.agent/decisions/ADR-026-operational-mode-design-PROPOSED.md:21-28`).

Therefore the validation helper is category (b): a pilot that is optimizable, but only after extraction. The pilot is incompatible with GPU-optimized core if kept as `coupled_step.py` plus snapshot dictionaries. The same composition semantics are compatible if moved into `dynamics/core/` as pure JAX scan bodies with mode parameters for savepoint collection, precision policy, solver selection, and boundary cadence. The evidence against category (a) is that operational already depends on the same pure primitives and has D2H-clean static scans (`src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:23-28`, `.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-rk1-d2h-acceptance/worker-report.md:23-32`). The evidence against direct pivot is that validation wrappers still own fp64 strictness, snapshots, offsets, and validation-only classifications (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b6-coupled-step-parity/worker-report.md:27-39`, `.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b6-coupled-step-parity/worker-report.md:83-87`).

# §4 — Proposed restructure

Recommendation path: reframe to shared core, not validation-as-operational.

Proposed modules:

- `src/gpuwrf/dynamics/core/acoustic.py`: `AcousticCoreState`, `advance_mu_t_core`, `w_solve_core`, `acoustic_substep_core`, and `acoustic_scan_core`. This owns field conventions for `mu`, `theta`, `mudf`, `muts`, `muave`, `ww`, `ph_tend`, `t_2ave`, `u_1/v_1/ww_1`, and the mass-coupled momentum inputs. Both validation and operational import it.
- `src/gpuwrf/dynamics/core/dycore.py`: `rk_stage_core` and `dycore_timestep_core`, with stage schedule as data. Validation can request WRF savepoint snapshots; operational can request no snapshots and static/lax scan lowering.
- `src/gpuwrf/dynamics/core/coupled.py`: `coupled_timestep_core` that composes dycore, physics, and boundary with explicit cadence and lead-time policy.
- `src/gpuwrf/dynamics/validation_wrappers.py`: current `acoustic_loop.py`, `dycore_step.py`, and `coupled_step.py` behavior becomes wrappers that call core and emit savepoint-shaped dictionaries. These wrappers remain validation-only.
- `src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py`: becomes a production wrapper that initializes resident carry, passes operational precision/solver/fusion policy to core, segments radiation for D2H cleanliness, donates buffers, and omits snapshots.

This collapses the known five defects because the risky facts are represented once. Missing scratch carry disappears because the core state enumerates exactly the fields consumed by the acoustic recurrence, while wrappers decide which are persistent versus local (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/acoustic_loop.py:32-47`, `src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_state.py:31-59`). RK1 acoustic omission disappears because the RK schedule is shared, not retyped once in validation and once in operational (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-operational-composition-bisection/worker-report.md:41-51`). Advance-mu-t commit, W coefficient cadence, `dt_sub`, and `ph_tend` formula disappear as separate copy points because `acoustic_substep_core` owns the commit semantics and tests can compare validation and operational wrappers at that boundary (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-rk1-sanity-check/sanity_memo.md:74-87`, `.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-real-ic-bisection/worker-report.md:19-28`). The fifth raw-velocity defect disappears only if the core interface requires mass-coupled momentum inputs or constructs them internally; the current operational call site passes raw `state.u` and `state.v` into `AdvanceMuTInputs` (`src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:270-304`), while the worker identified WRF's call as `ru_m/rv_m/ww_m` (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-fix-advance-mu-t-commit/worker-report.md:27-30`).

The one-sprint implementation plan should be narrow. First, add core dataclasses and pure functions by moving, not rewriting, the existing validated acoustic substep and RK composition. Second, make validation wrappers call the core and prove B6 golden remains `0.0`. Third, make operational call the same acoustic core for a one-step real-IC comparator, with the solver and precision still set to validation-equivalent values. Fourth, only after parity at that boundary, re-enable operational segmentation/D2H proof and 10 s bounded probe. No PCR, downcast expansion, or 1 h acceptance should be in that first reframe sprint.

The restructure should also document Amendment #1 supersession explicitly. The old rule was “operational does not import validation `acoustic_loop.py`/`dycore_step.py`/`coupled_step.py`.” The new rule should be “validation and operational may import `dynamics.core`, but validation wrappers may not be imported by operational runtime.” That keeps `PROJECT_PLAN.md:272` intact: pilots that cannot be optimized away are still wrong. It simply stops treating a validated pure recurrence as a pilot merely because the first user of it was the savepoint ladder.

# §5 — Risk of staying the course

If the next sprint only fixes `ru_m/rv_m/ww_m`, I estimate two to four remaining defect rounds before an honest M6b close attempt, with a high variance tail. The reason is the number of still-visible interface mismatches between `operational_mode.py` and the validation composition.

Concrete mismatches already visible in code:

1. Momentum inputs: operational passes raw `state.u`/`state.v` and `carry.ww` to `AdvanceMuTInputs` (`src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:270-304`), while the latest worker says WRF passes mass-coupled `ru_m/rv_m/ww_m` (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b-fix-advance-mu-t-commit/worker-report.md:27-30`).
2. RK/acoustic schedule: validation `dycore_timestep_wrf` calls `acoustic_loop_wrf` with `substeps=cfg.acoustic_substeps` for each of three RK stages (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/dycore_step.py:76-82`); operational uses RK1=1, RK2=half, RK3=full (`src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:384-393`). This may be WRF-correct operationally, but it means the locked validation comparator and operational path do not share one schedule contract.
3. Coefficient cadence: validation `acoustic_loop_wrf` computes `calc_coef_w_wrf_coefficients` once before the substep loop (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/acoustic_loop.py:233-244`); operational computes it inside every `_wrf_small_step_acoustic` call (`src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:305-315`).
4. Boundary lead time: validation computes `lead_seconds = max(step_index - 1, 0) * dt` (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/coupled_step.py:215-223`); operational uses `step_index * dt` (`src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:412-414`).
5. Physics sequence and cadence: validation calls Thompson, MYNN, then RRTMG every coupled step (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/coupled_step.py:188-192`); operational calls Thompson, MYNN, surface, and conditional RRTMG after static segmentation (`src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:404-415`, `src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:454-488`).
6. Thermodynamic offsets: validation explicitly adds theta and pressure offsets for physics state construction (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/coupled_step.py:126-138`); operational relies on the resident `State` and a heuristic `_theta_base_offset` in the acoustic path (`src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:162-165`, `src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:263-267`).
7. Precision: validation casts coupled state leaves to fp64 (`src/gpuwrf/dynamics/coupled_step.py:118-121`); operational enforces ADR-007 dtypes every step (`src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:154-159`, `src/gpuwrf/runtime/operational_mode.py:415`).

Some of these are intentional operational differences and should survive. The risk is that they are mixed with accidental differences in the same file. Staying the course can work only with a kill gate: after the `ru_m/rv_m/ww_m` fix, require step-1 real-IC operational-vs-core parity at the first acoustic substep, then 10 s bounded probe, then warmed D2H. If another first-substep interface defect appears, stop and reframe immediately.

My numeric estimate is therefore: one round for the named mass-coupled momentum fix, one likely round for boundary/physics cadence or lead-time mismatch once 1 h is reached, and one likely round for precision/offset conventions when Tier-4 is finally run. A fourth round is plausible if the validation RK schedule and operational WRF schedule remain different enough that the comparator keeps reporting intentional differences as blockers. The optimistic case is one more sprint; the median case is three; the kill gate should fire on the second new unnamed interface defect.

# §6 — Recommendation

Recommendation: `REFRAME-TO-SHARED-CORE`.

Supersede the operational rule narrowly: do not import validation wrappers into production, but do require validation and operational wrappers to import one shared pure numerical core for coupled timestep composition. This preserves the principal directive because savepoints, Python diagnostics, HDF5 layout, fp64 strictness, and snapshot dictionaries remain validation-only (`PROJECT_PLAN.md:254-266`, `/home/enric/.claude/projects/-home-enric-src-wrf-gpu2/memory/feedback_gpu_optimized_core_primacy.md:21-26`). It also addresses the empirical failure: the current “compose own variants” rule has produced duplicate composition code with at least five discovered defects and several visible remaining interface mismatches, while B6 validation remains the only bitwise-locked truth source (`.agent/sprints/2026-05-25-m6b6-coupled-step-parity/worker-report.md:17-24`).

The one-sprint plan is: create `dynamics/core` with pure acoustic/RK/coupled functions; convert validation wrappers to call it and reprove B6 `0.0`; convert operational to call the same acoustic/RK core with validation-equivalent precision for the real-IC step-1 comparator; then rerun 10 s bounds and warmed D2H. Do not claim M6b, speedup, PCR, or mixed-precision success in that sprint. The proof object should be a three-way table: validation wrapper vs core, operational wrapper vs core, and warmed D2H summary.

Dissent: `STAY-THE-COURSE` is not irrational. The project has good bisection infrastructure, D2H is now clean, and the fifth defect has a plausible concrete fix. A manager could dispatch `ru_m/rv_m/ww_m`, demand a hard kill gate, and perhaps close the current cascade faster than a restructure. The reason I reject that as primary recommendation is that the next success would still leave two independently composed timesteps. The project would have paid for another patch without reducing the class of failures that caused the cascade.

I also reject `PIVOT-TO-VALIDATION-AS-OPERATIONAL`. It would maximize short-term correctness confidence, but it would violate the spirit of §14.5.1 by making snapshot-shaped, fp64, validation-owned composition the production dycore before proving fusion, memory layout, and precision escape hatches (`PROJECT_PLAN.md:256-266`). `HYBRID` is a reasonable fallback if the manager believes a full core extraction is too disruptive: share only the RK/acoustic composition template while keeping operator implementations separate. I rank it second. It reduces the RK1 and cadence defect class, but it still leaves the advance-mu-t commit, momentum-interface, and scratch-state semantics duplicated. `REFRAME-TO-SHARED-CORE` is the smallest change that removes the whole defect class while preserving GPU-optimized wrappers.
